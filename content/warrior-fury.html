Fury Warrior Leveling Guide

Dual-wield speed, high APM, and strong scaling. Covers Classic, TBC, and WotLK Fury leveling with talents, rotations, weapons, and routing for 1-80.

Updated 2026 Dual-Wield DPS Supports Classic / TBC / WotLK

Why Fury in Classic?

Once Dual Wield is available and you secure solid weapons, Fury offers fast, consistent damage. Earlier levels prefer 2H Arms; swap to Fury around 40+ with good dual-wields.

Strengths

  • Fast kill speed with Flurry/Crit.
  • Bloodthirst sustain; rage generation snowballs with gear.
  • Great dungeon DPS when weapons are strong.

Weaknesses

  • Highly weapon dependent; weak if dual-wields are bad.
  • Less control than Arms; no big burst CC.
  • Can be rage-starved on single weak hits.

Talents by Level (Classic 1-60)

Early (10-39)

  • Level as Arms to Mortal Strike; Fury off-tree for Cruelty/Enrage.
  • Pick up Dual Wield at 20; use if weapons are good.
  • Flurry 5/5 when reachable (requires respec to Fury at 40).

Mid (40-49)

  • Bloodthirst at 40 (Fury core); Improved Whirlwind for cleave.
  • Precision for hit; Enrage 5/5 for damage spikes.
  • Dual-wield slow/fast or slow/slow depending on gear.

Late (50-60)

  • Armored to the Teeth (not in Classic) skip; fill Commanding Presence/Imp Battle Shout.
  • Arms off-tree: Tactical Mastery, Impale if not taken.
  • Optional: Improved Berserker Rage for rage injection.
Switch to Fury at 40+ when you have strong dual-wields (e.g., Thrash Blade + Dal’Rend main-hand set) and enough hit.

Rotation & Playstyle

Single Target

  • Maintain Battle Shout; Bloodthirst on cooldown.
  • Whirlwind on CD even single target (Classic threat-aware).
  • Execute sub 20%; Heroic Strike as rage dump.

Cleave

  • Whirlwind + Cleave spam with Flurry up.
  • Sweeping Strikes (if Arms hybrid) for extra cleave.
  • Use Demo Shout/Thunder Clap for safety when overpulling.

Survival

  • Bandage/eat between pulls; keep First Aid maxed.
  • Use Berserker Rage for fear immunity; Intimidating Shout reset.
  • Shield swap for Spell Reflect/Shield Block when needed.

Why Fury in TBC?

Dual-wield scaling explodes with Outland weapons, Flurry uptime, and Windfury totem synergy. Fury excels in dungeons and remains a strong questing spec.

Spec: 17+ Arms for Impale/Deep Wounds, then Fury for Bloodthirst, Rampage, and Flurry. Precision for hit; Improved Berserker Rage for rage injection.

Talents by Level (TBC 60-70)

Early (60-64)

  • Arms: Deflection/Impale/Deep Wounds.
  • Fury: Cruelty 5/5, Unbridled Wrath (optional), Blood Craze 3/3 for sustain.

Mid (65-67)

  • Bloodthirst core; Improved Whirlwind; Flurry 5/5.
  • Precision 3/3 for hit; Commanding Presence for group utility.

Late (68-70)

  • Rampage 1/1; Improved Berserker Rage.
  • Filler: Dual Wield Specialization, Imp Battle Shout.
  • Optional: Piercing Howl for control.

Rotation (TBC)

Single Target

  • Bloodthirst on CD; Whirlwind on CD; Execute sub 20%.
  • Keep Rampage up; Heroic Strike as rage dump when >60 rage.
  • Maintain Battle Shout; use Death Wish/Trinkets for elites.

AoE/Dungeons

  • Whirlwind + Cleave; Piercing Howl for control if talented.
  • Demo Shout/Thunder Clap for safety when tank weak.
  • Use Bloodrage/Berserker Rage to sustain rage between packs.

Survival

  • Shield swap for Spell Reflect/Shield Block on casters.
  • Use Intercept/Intervene mobility; Bandages/food keep uptime.
  • Health pot + Rage potions on tough elites.

Why Fury in WotLK?

Fury shines with Titan’s Grip or dual 1H (pre-60). Endless rage with Bloodthirst/Whirlwind loop, strong Execute via Sudden Death (if taken), and great scaling.

Spec: Fury deep to Bloodthirst, Improved Whirlwind, Rampage, Titan’s Grip at 60 talent points (WotLK). Off-tree Arms: Impale/Deep Wounds. Pick Precision, Flurry, Furious Attacks if PvP.

Talents by Level (70-80)

Early (70-74)

  • Improved Berserker Rage, Precision, Flurry, Armored to the Teeth.
  • Bloodthirst core; Improved Whirlwind.
  • Arms off-tree: Impale/Deep Wounds for bleed/crit.

Mid (75-77)

  • Rampage; Bloodsurge (Slam proc) for mobility.
  • Intensify Rage for shorter CDs; Furious Attacks optional.
  • Fill Commanding Presence/Imp Shout as needed.

Late (78-80)

  • Titan’s Grip for dual 2H (endgame) or Single-Minded Fury (not in WotLK; TG is key).
  • Heroic Fury for mobility; Improved Cleave for AoE.
  • Slam (Bloodsurge) usage—instant, don’t clip Whirlwind/Bloodthirst.

Rotation (WotLK)

Single Target

  • Bloodthirst → Whirlwind priority; Slam on Bloodsurge procs.
  • Heroic Strike/Cleave as rage dump (queue if >60 rage).
  • Keep Battle Shout; use Death Wish (if talented)/Trinkets.

AoE

  • Whirlwind on CD; Cleave spam; Improved Cleave helps.
  • Bladestorm belongs to Arms—Fury relies on Whirlwind/Cleave.
  • Thunder Clap + Demo Shout if tanking small pulls.

Survival

  • Bloodthirst self-heal; Enraged Regeneration if available.
  • Shield Wall/Spell Reflect when swapping to shield.
  • Piercing Howl for control (if talented); Intimidating Shout reset.

Gear, Weapons, and Enchants (All Versions)

Stat Priority

  • Hit (6-9% target while leveling) > Strength > Crit > Armor Pen (WotLK) > Haste.
  • Stamina for safety; Agility leather/mail if higher DPS stats.

Weapons

  • Classic: Thrash Blade, Dal’Rend set, Mirah’s Song.
  • TBC: Lantresor axes, Felsteel Longblade, Mag’hari Furybrand.
  • WotLK: 2x Titansteel weapons or crafted maces; slow/slow for Whirlwind/Cleave.

Enchants

  • Classic: Crusader/STR enchants; +Hit on gloves if available.
  • TBC: Savagery to 2H; +Str/+Hit to gloves; +Str to bracers.
  • WotLK: Berserking/Massacre to weapons; Icewalker if hit needed.

Pro Tips & Safety

Leveling Speed

  • Keep First Aid maxed; use bandages to save food time.
  • Use Berserker Rage for rage/fear immunity; Bloodrage on cooldown.
  • Engineer grenades for extra AoE and stuns.

Control

  • Hamstring kite tough mobs; Piercing Howl for packs.
  • Intercept/Charge to casters; LOS pulls in dungeons.
  • Shield swap for Reflect/Wall on dangerous spells.

Group Utility

  • Battle/Commanding Shout; Demo Shout/Thunder Clap for safety.
  • Sunder Armor stacks for party physical DPS.
  • Intervene to save healers; Rally threat with Taunt (if tanking temporarily).
