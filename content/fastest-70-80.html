Why Northrend Leveling Is Different

If you speedran 1-60 or 60-70, throw out half your habits. Northrend was Blizzard's first continent designed around quest hub density — you rarely travel more than a minute between hubs, quests chain into each other automatically, and vehicle/bombing quests hand out five-figure XP for 90 seconds of work. The result: raw quest XP per hour in Northrend beats grinding by a wider margin than any earlier bracket.

Three structural differences shape the entire route:

1. Two starting zones, one correct answer

Borean Tundra and Howling Fjord both cover 70-72, but Borean Tundra's hubs sit closer together and its breadcrumbs feed directly into Dragonblight. Fjord has better story; Tundra has better XP per hour. Speed levelers start Borean and only dip into Fjord if they outlevel content early (Horde: the Apothecary chain in Vengeance Landing is worth the detour).

2. No flying until 77

Unlike TBC, you're grounded for seven of ten levels. That makes zone order and flight-path discipline matter more — every hearthstone cooldown and mage portal is worth real minutes. At 77, Cold Weather Flying (1,000g) instantly changes the game for Storm Peaks and Icecrown.

3. Rested XP is worth more than ever

Northrend mobs give big kill XP, so double-XP rested time is enormous. Every log-out should happen in an inn or capital. A player who banks rested overnight between sessions can shave 4-5 hours off the total climb.

Pre-Northrend Preparation

Fifteen minutes of prep in Shattrath or your capital saves hours in Northrend. Do all of this before boarding the boat or zeppelin:

Gear & heirlooms

  • Heirloom shoulders + chest (10% XP each, 20% total) — buy with badges, honor, or Stone Keeper's Shards on alts.
  • Keep your TBC epics; Karazhan/S3-level gear carries you comfortably to 76-77 before Northrend blues overtake it.
  • A fast ground mount (100% speed minimum, epic ground strongly recommended).

Bags, consumables, and gold

  • Four 16-slot+ bags — Northrend quest items and vendor trash fill bags fast.
  • Food/water stacks for 10 levels; drums and scrolls if no buffer classes in group.
  • Bank 1,200g+ before you leave: 1,000g for Cold Weather Flying at 77, the rest for repairs and respecs.

Quest log setup

Clear your quest log except for dungeon quests you can share. Pick up the Nexus quests (Coldarra) and Drak'Tharon quests before their respective inserts — a full pre-collected dungeon quest set is worth 350k+ XP per cleave.

Recommended Zone Order

Both factions begin in the Borean Tundra boat/zeppelin for faster quest density, then swap continents at level 75 to keep XP flowing.

Alliance Route

  • 70-71: Borean Tundra (Fizzcrank & Riplash)
  • 71-73: Dragonblight (Wrathgate unlocked early)
  • 73-75: Grizzly Hills (Eversong dailies unlocked)
  • 75-77: Zul'Drak + Gundrak one-and-done
  • 77-80: Storm Peaks into Icecrown

Horde Route

  • 70-71: Borean Tundra (Warsong Offensive)
  • 71-73: Howling Fjord detour for Apothecary chain
  • 73-75: Dragonblight Wrathgate skip quest
  • 75-77: Zul'Drak + Drak'Tharon dungeon spam
  • 77-80: Icecrown gunship into Argent Vanguard hubs
Cold Weather Flying should be purchased immediately at 77 (borrow gold if needed). Flying cuts nearly 90 minutes from Storm Peaks + Icecrown loops.

Levels 70-72: Borean Tundra (& the Fjord Question)

Target: 4h 30m for the bracket. Take the boat from Stormwind (Alliance) or the zeppelin from Orgrimmar (Horde) to Borean Tundra. Both factions land in a fortress stuffed with quests — grab everything.

Alliance: Valiance Keep → Fizzcrank → Riplash

Clear the Valiance Keep starter batch (the Beryl Sorcerer chain and Nerub'ar attack quests overlap on the same mobs). Push west to Fizzcrank Airstrip — the gnome quests there are the densest hub in the zone, with the "harvest hut" mechanized chain giving near-continuous turn-ins. Finish south at Riplash Strand for the naga chain, then take the Kaskala/Unu'pe tuskarr quests on your way out.

Horde: Warsong Hold → Taunka'le → Bor'gorok

Warsong Hold's opening batch (Kaw the Mammoth Destroyer, the abomination quests inside the hold's basement) is huge — don't skip the indoor quests. Move to Taunka'le Village, then Bor'gorok Outpost in the north for the quick buffalo-and-flag quests. Horde routing is slightly faster here than Alliance — bank the spare minutes for the Fjord detour below.

💡 The D.E.H.T.A. loop is free XP

The druid camp on the northern road (both factions) has six kill/free-the-animals quests that all complete inside one small area. Ten minutes, ~55k XP with heirlooms. Do it whenever you pass by.

Horde only: Howling Fjord detour (71)

At 71, Horde should zeppelin to Vengeance Landing for the Apothecary chain and New Agamand hubs — roughly 40 minutes for 180k XP, and it banks the level cushion needed to enter Dragonblight at 71.5. Alliance skips Fjord entirely unless behind on XP.

⚠️ Don't finish Borean Tundra

The zone holds far more quests than you need. The moment the Dragonblight breadcrumb appears (level 71.5+), take it. Completionism here is the #1 pace-killer in the bracket.

Levels 72-75: Dragonblight → Grizzly Hills

Target: 9h for the bracket. This is the heart of the route — two of the best-designed quest zones Blizzard ever shipped, back to back.

Dragonblight (72-74)

Run the Nexus insert first (see Dungeon Cleave Inserts) — the Coldarra quests turn in inside Dragonblight's westward flow. Then: Stars' Rest / Agmar's Hammer as your base, the Moa'ki Harbor tuskarr coastal chain, and the Wyrmrest Temple dragon quests (the Aces High! flying-drake daily unlock is a permanent XP faucet).

The zone finale is the Wrathgate chain — do it. It's long-ish but the finale battle plus the follow-up capital-city event pays ~120k XP and unlocks follow-up hubs. Skipping Wrathgate to "save time" is a classic false economy.

Grizzly Hills (74-75)

Enter at Amberpine Lodge (Alliance) or Conquest Hold (Horde). The Venture Bay PvP-flavored quests are quick even on PvE realms, the Drakuru troll chain starts here (it follows you into Zul'Drak — pick it up now), and the logging-camp loops are tight kill-and-collect clusters. Grizzly Hills is short by design: you only need ~70% of the zone before Zul'Drak's breadcrumb fires at 75.

💡 Mail your turn-in items forward

Quest items like Everfrost Chips and collected relics sit in bags for hours. If an alt or friend can mail consumables to Northrend inns, keep bag pressure low — vendor trips in Dragonblight cost 5+ minutes each.

Levels 75-77: Zul'Drak (or Sholazar)

Target: 7h for the bracket. Zul'Drak is dense but grim — Argent Stand and the Ebon Watch chains flow well, and the Drakuru storyline you started in Grizzly Hills concludes here with big XP payouts. Run the Drak'Tharon Keep insert at 76 with both zones' dungeon quests collected.

The Amphitheater of Anguish

Zul'Drak's ring-of-blood style arena chain (group of 3-5 recommended) pays ~180k XP for 20 minutes of boss tag-teaming. LFG spam for it — everyone in the zone wants it.

Alternative: Sholazar Basin (76-78)

If Zul'Drak's second half feels slow (it is), fly to Sholazar Basin instead. The Nesingwary Base Camp hubs are almost entirely kill quests on high-density mobs, the zone is compact, and the pillar-of-life chain routes cleanly. Speed runs often split: Zul'Drak 75-76.5, Sholazar 76.5-77.5.

⚠️ Skip the fishing/reputation side quests

The Oracles vs Frenzyheart faction choice at the end of Sholazar is a level-cap activity. Do the quests leading to it, take the XP, and leave the egg-farming for level 80.

Levels 77-80: Storm Peaks → Icecrown

Target: 11h for the bracket (the XP-per-level curve steepens sharply here). Buy Cold Weather Flying the second you ding 77 — both remaining zones are vertically designed and barely function on the ground.

Storm Peaks (77-79)

Start at K3, work up to the Brunnhildar Village chain — yes, you get turned into a Hyldnir woman for two hours, and yes, it's the fastest quest flow in the zone. It ends at the Sons of Hodir unlock, which converts into your daily XP faucet (see Daily Planning). The Sifreldar/Valkyrion harpoon-and-drake quests that follow are all vehicle quests — huge XP, zero downtime.

Icecrown (78/79-80)

Icecrown is gated behind short attunement-style intro chains at the Argent Vanguard and Death's Rise. The zone is entirely hub-gated — each quest cluster unlocks the next — so you can't cherry-pick. Follow the flow: Argent Vanguard → Crusaders' Pinnacle → Aldur'thar assaults. The bombing-run and gunship quests pay 25-30k XP each for under two minutes of play.

💡 Ding 80 and stop

You'll hit 80 with roughly a third of Icecrown untouched. Bank those quests — at level 80 they pay out ~13g each and fund your Cold Weather Flying debt, epic flying, or first repair bills in heroics.

Dungeon Cleave Inserts

One well-timed dungeon marathon is still the fastest way to convert rested XP while aligning quest hubs.

Level 72 Boost

  • 2x Nexus clear with all quests picked up beforehand.
  • Focus on blue weapon upgrades + off-hands.
  • Turn-ins deliver ~380k XP (Alliance) or 360k (Horde).

Level 76 Boost

  • Drak'Tharon Keep spam with double group quests shared.
  • Prioritize casters for Mojo Frenzy event achievements.
  • Leads directly into ZD final chains without travel.

Advanced Speed Tips

Hearthstone choreography

Set your hearth to the zone you're about to finish, not the one you're in. Finish the last hub, hearth back for turn-ins, take the flight path forward. Every hearth used on cooldown saves 3-6 minutes of backtracking; over ten levels that's an hour.

Log out in inns — always

Rested XP doubles kill XP, and Northrend kill XP is a large share of your total. If your session ends mid-field, spend the 60 seconds to reach an inn. Multi-session levelers gain 4-5 hours from this alone.

Vehicle and bombing quests are never skips

Any quest that puts you in a vehicle, on a drake, or behind a turret pays disproportionate XP for the time spent. When triaging a hub, do vehicle quests first, group quests last.

Group quests: tag, don't wait

Northrend group quests (Amphitheater of Anguish, elite assists in Icecrown) are massive XP but only with an instant group. Advertise while questing nearby; if no group forms in 5 minutes, move on and come back.

Train every two levels

Wrath ability ranks are significant (especially healer/caster base spells). Plan trainer visits to coincide with hearth turn-in trips — never make a dedicated training trip.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does 70-80 take in WotLK Classic?

With heirloom shoulders and chest, full rested XP, and tight zone routing, expect 34-36 hours played. Casual questing without heirlooms lands closer to 45-50 hours. Dungeon-cleave groups with a dedicated tank can push under 30 hours.

Should I quest or spam dungeons from 70-80?

Solo, questing is fastest — Northrend quest density is the best in Classic. Dungeon spam only wins with a premade cleave group and a geared tank. The optimal hybrid is questing with two timed dungeon inserts: Nexus at 72 and Drak'Tharon Keep at 76, with all dungeon quests collected beforehand.

When should I buy Cold Weather Flying?

Immediately at level 77 — it costs 1,000 gold and pays for itself in saved travel time within a session. Storm Peaks and Icecrown are built around flying; grounding yourself there costs roughly 90 extra minutes.

What is the fastest class to level 70-80 in WotLK?

Death Knight is the fastest thanks to self-healing, strong AoE, and no downtime. Retribution Paladin, Beast Mastery Hunter, and Affliction Warlock are close behind. Frost Mage AoE grinding is viable but weaker than in vanilla brackets.

Is Sholazar Basin worth questing?

Yes — Sholazar (76-78) is the best-kept secret of the bracket. The Nesingwary hub chains are dense, mostly kill quests with high mob density, and the zone is compact. Use it to replace the slower second half of Zul'Drak if you fall behind pace.

Do I need to finish Icecrown before hitting 80?

No. Stop questing the moment you ding 80 and switch to daily hubs (Sons of Hodir, Argent Vanguard, Ebon Blade) and heroic dungeons. Save the remaining Icecrown quests as gold income at max level.

Final Thoughts

The 70-80 bracket rewards discipline over heroics: start Borean, leave zones the moment breadcrumbs fire, insert Nexus at 72 and Drak'Tharon at 76 with quests pre-collected, buy flying at 77 without hesitation, and stop questing the instant you ding 80. Follow that skeleton and sub-35 hours is very achievable on any class.
