The Legend of Joana's Guide

To understand this comparison, you need to know Joana's place in WoW history. In 2005, a player named Joana (formerly Mancow) achieved the impossible: hitting level 60 in just 4 days and 20 hours of /played time, setting a world record that stood for years.

Joana documented the entire journey and released it as a comprehensive text-based leveling guide. This guide became the gold standard for speed leveling in vanilla WoW and early Classic. Thousands of players used Joana's precise instructions to level their characters efficiently.

Historical Impact: Joana's guide literally defined optimal leveling routes for an entire generation of WoW players. Many modern guides, including RestedXP, build upon the foundation Joana established nearly 20 years ago.

Why Joana's Guide Mattered

Before Joana, leveling guides were vague and inefficient. Players wandered between zones, picked up random quests, and frequently got stuck without clear direction. Joana changed everything by providing step-by-step instructions optimized for pure speed.

The guide covered every level from 1-60 for Horde, with precise quest orders, grinding spots, and travel routes. It was revolutionary for its time and remained the best option for over a decade.

But That Was 2005...

The gaming landscape has changed dramatically since Joana's guide first launched. In 2005, alt-tabbing to read text guides was normal. In 2026, players expect in-game navigation, automatic tracking, and real-time updates. This is where modern solutions like RestedXP excel.
